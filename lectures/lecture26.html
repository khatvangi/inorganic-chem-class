Oxygen transport and storage

Dioxygen, O2, is a special molecule that has not always been

available to biology; in fact, to many life forms it is highly

toxic. The early atmosphere on Earth had no O2: indeed, as

the waste product of oxygenic photosynthesis that began

with cyanobacteria more than 2 Ga ago, O2 is a biogenic

substance (Section 16.4), owing its existence to solar energy

capture by living organisms. As we shall see in Section

26.10, the great thermodynamic advantage of having such

a powerful oxidant available undoubtedly led to the evolu­
