An interesting class of compounds is the interhalogens

which are formed between Group 17 elements in a way

not observed in other groups. The binary interhalogens are

molecular compounds with formulas XY, XY3, XY5, and

XY7, where the heavier, less electronegative halogen X is the

central atom. They also form ternary interhalogens of the

type XY2Z and XYZ2, where Z is also a halogen atom. The

interhalogens are of special importance as highly reactive

intermediates and for providing useful insights into bonding.
