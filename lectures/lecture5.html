Simple oxoacids

The simplest oxoacids are the mononuclear acids, which con­

tain one atom of the parent element. They include H2CO3,

HNO3, H3PO4, and H2SO4.3 These oxoacids are formed by

the electronegative elements at the upper right of the peri­

odic table and by other elements in high oxidation states

(Table 5.3). One interesting feature in the table is the occur­

rence of planar H2CO3 and HNO3 molecules but not their

analogues in later periods. As we saw in Chapter 2, pπ–pπ

bonding is more important among the Period 2 elements, so

their atoms are more likely to be constrained to lie in a plane.
